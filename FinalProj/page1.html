About

My project involves the analysis of different colour palettes used in Japanese animated films through the different genres. It will consist of colour analysis of the following categories: Studio Ghibli, Cyberpunk Science Fiction, Modern Era Anime films. The website will consist of some background knowledge of colour theory in films and anime, colour palette preference questionnaire and the colour analysis of the websites. In many visual art mediums, such as film, colours are utilised to emphasise certain emotions and moods. According to Digital Synopsis, colour psychology has often been implemented in films, especially animated ones, to subconsciously evoke specific emotions among the audience. Anime is a highly respected form of animated film, partly due to iconic films such as Spirited Away, Neon Genesis Evangelion and Your Name. By analysing their use of colours and colour theory, we can further understand and apply these skills into future pieces of art and film.

Introduction to the use of colours

Colour, an understated yet very important aspect of any creative medium. Whether it is film, photography, graphics design, paintings or even a simple drawing with colour pencils. In any artistic, visual medium, colours are used to accentuate different tone, moods and express the emotions that certain scenes are trying to convey in a subtle way. Colours symbolise different emotions and messages, with some colours having different meanings based off the context or characteristics of the colour (saturation, hue) such as:


Pink - innocence, romance, femininity, beauty

Red - love, passion, anger, danger

Orange - humour, warmth, friendliness, youth

Yellow - attention, wisdom, idealisation, naviety

Green - nature, healing, envy, corruption

Blue - isolation, melancholy, calm, spirituality, loyalty

Purple - fantasy, ethereal, mysticism, nobility

Colour Schemes

Most common colour schemes are:

Complementary: opposite colours on the colour wheel -> contrast can make imagery stand out

Analogue: colours next to each other on the colour wheel -> colour harmony, contains dominate, supporting and accent colours

Triadic: evenly spaced around the colour wheel -> visual contrast and harmony

Use of RGB values for colour analysis:

The RGB colour model is an additive colour model in which the red, green and blue primary colours of light are added together in various ways to reproduce a broad array of colours. RGB values typically consist of 3 main values as well as an additional fourth value indicating opacity. The first value represents the red value, the second represents the green value and the third represents the blue value. The range for all the values are from 0 to 255. The RGB color model uses three values to specify the intensity of red, green, and blue light in a color. These values range from 0 to 255, where 0 represents no intensity, and 255 represents the maximum intensity for that color.

An example of how RGB code is represented is:

White - rgb (255, 255, 255)

Black - rgb (0, 0, 0)

Cyan - rgb (0, 100, 100) which is an even mixture of Blue and Green at 100 units

The 3 components to Colour

Saturation - intensity or purity of a colour
A colour with equal amounts of red green and blue (eg. rgb 128, 128, 128) will be fully saturated while colours with only one colour component (eg. 255, 0, 0 for pure red) will be highly saturated. Decreasing the values of red, blue and green equally will result in less saturation, as decreasing the values will make the colours less vibrant.

Hue- the colour itself, its position on the colour wheel
It is determined by the ratio of red, green and blue values. For example, (rgb 128, 128, 0) having equal parts red and green results in a yellow hue.

Value - brightness of a colour
Values are determined by the sum of red, green and blue values, with higher values resulting in brighter colours. White is the brightest colour with (rgb 255, 255, 255). Multiplying each colour by the same factor will lighten (whole number) / darken (decimal) the colour while maintaining its hue and saturation.
