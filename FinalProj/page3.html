Studio Ghibli

Introduction to Studio Ghibli

The brilliance of Studio Ghibli films are largely attributed to Studio Ghibli's co-founder and director of many of its films - Hayao Miyazaki. Studio Ghibli is well known for its high quality film-making and artistry. Studio Ghibli has revolutionised the way animation has been perceived by audience and the potential animation as a medium as, changing the public's perception of animated movies from merely for children to a medium for all ages through the quality storytelling and animation in many of its films.

Miyazaki's films offer audiences a form of escapism in their fantastical worlds, whilst also touching on topics such as environmentalism and relationships, perfectly balancing the spectacle of its fantasy elements with its realism. Miyazaki has truly proven himself to be a phenomenal director and master of space and time with the ability to create empathetic characters. It is no wonder Studio Ghibli's films have captured the hearts of a worldwide audience and developed a loyal audience.

However, it is not only the story elements that make Studio Ghibli's films a piece of art, but also the deliberate and poetic use of colours. This contribution was given by the talented animator and colour designer - the late Michiyo Yasuda. To quote Michiyo Yasuda

"Color has a meaning and it makes the film more easily understood. Colours and pictures can enhance what the situation is on screen. Viewers inevitably form associations between certain colours and feelings"

With its delightful characters, stunning animation, and heartwarming story, "Kiki's Delivery Service" has become a beloved classic for audiences of all ages.

90s Cyperpunk

Introduction to 90s Cyberpunk

In the 1990s, cyberpunk anime rose to popularity with its themes of dystopian futures, advanced technology, and gritty urban environments. These anime explored the relationship between humans and technology, often featuring stories that revolved around hackers, cyborgs, and artificial intelligence.

90s cyberpunk anime has had a significant impact on science fiction and anime. It introduced a new subgenre of science fiction that explored the relationship between humans and technology in a dystopian society. It highlighted the potential dangers of a future where technology is ubiquitous and can have an adverse impact on human society.Many of the themes explored in 90s cyberpunk anime, such as artificial intelligence, cyborgs, and virtual reality, have become a staple in science fiction and have influenced other media forms such as movies, video games, and literature. This subgenre also explored complex philosophical themes such as the nature of humanity, consciousness, and morality, and challenged the traditional definitions of what it means to be human.

Moreover, 90s cyberpunk anime had a significant impact on the anime industry, as it pushed the boundaries of traditional anime storytelling and animation. It introduced a darker and more mature storytelling style, and the animation was often more detailed and sophisticated, which has set the standard for future anime production.

Overall, the impact of 90s cyberpunk anime can still be felt today, as its influence has transcended its time and has become a part of the cultural zeitgeist.

As Tetsuo struggles to control his newfound abilities, he becomes embroiled in a power struggle between the government, a secret military project, and a group of psychic children led by his friend Kaneda. The film explores themes of power, corruption, and humanity's relationship with technology, and features stunning animation and a memorable soundtrack.

Ghost in the Shell

Summary

"Ghost in the Shell" is a 1995 Japanese animated science-fiction film directed by Mamoru Oshii. The story is set in a future world where humans have cyberbrains and can connect to networks with their minds. The main character, Major Motoko Kusanagi, is a cyborg who works for Section 9, a special-operations task force assigned to combat cyber-terrorism and other high-tech crimes.

When a powerful hacker known as the Puppet Master begins infiltrating the minds of key government officials and manipulating their thoughts, Major Kusanagi and her team are tasked with tracking down the elusive criminal. As they investigate, they discover that the Puppet Master may not be a criminal at all, but rather a sentient program that has gained self-awareness and is seeking a way to become fully human. As Major Kusanagi delves deeper into the Puppet Master's motives, she begins to question her own existence and what it means to be human in a world where technology and the human mind are becoming increasingly intertwined. The film explores themes of identity, consciousness, and the nature of existence in a rapidly changing world.

"Ghost in the Shell" is widely regarded as a classic of the cyberpunk genre and has had a significant impact on science fiction and anime. It has inspired numerous adaptations and spin-offs, including a 2017 live-action film adaptation starring Scarlett Johansson.

New Age

Introduction to New Age Anime

"New Age" modern anime generally refers to anime produced in the late 2000s to the present day. This period is characterized by significant changes in the anime industry, including the rise of digital animation and streaming services.

One of the defining features of "New Age" modern anime is the diversity of genres and themes. While anime has always featured a wide range of genres, modern anime has expanded to include more unconventional and experimental storytelling, as well as more diverse representation of characters and themes. Another notable feature of "New Age" modern anime is the influence of social media and online fan communities. Anime producers are increasingly aware of the importance of fan engagement and often incorporate fan feedback and expectations into their storytelling and character development.

In terms of animation, "New Age" modern anime often features highly detailed and fluid animation, often incorporating 3D animation and motion capture technology. This allows for more dynamic and intricate action scenes and greater visual fidelity overall.

Overall, "New Age" modern anime represents a continuation and evolution of the anime industry, incorporating new technologies and storytelling techniques while retaining the unique artistic and narrative qualities that have made anime a beloved and enduring art form.

Belle

Summary

"Belle" is a 2021 Japanese animated film directed by Mamoru Hosoda. The movie is set in a world called "U," where a popular virtual world called "Another" is used to escape reality. The story follows a 17-year-old high school girl named Suzu, who lives in a rural town and has a talent for singing. She enters "Another" and becomes "Belle," a virtual superstar singer who captivates fans all over the world. However, as her popularity grows, Suzu begins to question the true nature of "Another" and the authenticity of her online persona. Meanwhile, a mysterious creature threatens the virtual world and Suzu must use her singing to save it. The movie explores themes of identity, self-discovery, and the relationship between humans and technology.

This scene from Belle utilises an analogous colour scheme, with its main colours being blue, purple and red, which are next to each other in the colour wheel. The setting of the night sky and the mysterious creature are of a darker to mid value colours, such as dark cyan (33, 68, 88), resulting in lower to mid rgb values. However, Belle has a midtone to pastel colour scheme, consisting of colours of mid to high rgb values such as pastel purple (rgb 225, 185, 234). This allow for Belle to stand out more in the scene as well as serve the purposes of being the light and hope in the monster's eyes.

Another observation that could be made is that gradients are heavily used in this scene, resulting in the rgb values of certain colours to be similar, such as pastel orange-red (rgb 244, 153, 160) and midtone red (170, 57, 87) as the red has the highest value, followed by blue and lastly green for both colours.
